Seattle Regulatory RAG · 2026

A regulatory question, answered with a real citation.

A planner runs the numbers on a parking lot. A land-use attorney drafts a memo on middle-housing parking. A policy analyst needs to know where a new state rule preempts local code. Each of them has the same first problem: which agency's rule actually governs, and what does it literally say.

The rules live across eight bodies (WAC, RCW, SMC, Director's Rules, IBC-WA, WA Court Opinions, Governor's Orders, SPU Design Standards), in 5,208 PDFs and 89,855 sections. They cross-reference each other, contradict each other, and quietly renumber. No human reads them all.

The pattern is consistent across the gold set: a public LLM produces fluent prose with confident citations that do not survive a check against the actual PDF. This system answers with text and citations that did come from the actual PDF, refuses when retrieval came up empty, and corrects false premises before answering.
