In Seattle's sanitary sewer system, a private side sewer carries wastewater from a building to the publicly maintained sewer main in the street. Under the Seattle Director's Rule governing the design and construction of side sewers, what is the minimum diameter for a side sewer on private property that serves between two and eight dwelling units? And under Seattle Public Utilities' Design Standards, what is the minimum diameter required for the public sanitary sewer main that the side sewer discharges into?

Side sewer (private, serving two to eight dwelling units): minimum 6 inches in diameter, set by Seattle Director's Rule 4-2011 (Part V, Section C.2).
